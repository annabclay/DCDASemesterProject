Conclusion

Answering the Research Question and Future Implications

How This Research Answers the Research Question

Research Question: How is emotional vulnerability strategically performed or edited in reality television to increase audience engagement and monetization?

Complete Answer

Our project shows that emotional moments in reality TV are often shaped by producers and editing, rather than presented as fully raw experiences. These moments are central to how shows attract attention and encourage audiences to watch, react, and share, especially across social media platforms.

By comparing what appears in episodes with how audiences respond online, we found a clear connection between storytelling choices and engagement outcomes. This supports our argument that vulnerability is not only represented but also strategically constructed for visibility and reach.

Our findings also raise ethical concerns, since emotionally vulnerable moments can be edited and circulated in ways that serve production goals. Future work could strengthen this study by collecting additional audience-response data and expanding analysis across more shows and formats. Overall, this project provides a strong understanding of how emotional vulnerability functions in contemporary reality television.

What We Learned

1. Emotional moments are shaped, not just captured

Across The Bachelor, Love Island, The Traitors, and Dance Moms, we saw that producers and editors help build emotional scenes instead of only documenting raw reality.

2. Editing choices strongly affect audience interpretation

One of our clearest examples came from The Traitors, where voiceover and reaction shots made a moment feel more dramatic than what appeared to happen in real time.

3. Social media amplifies vulnerable scenes

We were surprised by how often emotional clips are reposted and spread on TikTok and Instagram, making the connection between vulnerability and engagement much more visible.

Challenges and Limitations

Method Challenge

  • Subjectivity: It is easy to label a scene as emotional, but harder to explain exactly how editing techniques produced that feeling.
  • Organization: Grouping emotional moments across different shows into clear categories took multiple revisions.

Project Scope Limitations

  • Scale: We focused on selected clips and a small set of shows, so this is a strong starting pattern rather than a complete map of all reality TV.
  • Website constraints: Presenting all findings in a clean way was challenging while trying not to overwhelm the audience with too much text.

Project Successes

Clear direction and completed core dataset

We finalized our research question, collected our clips, and identified consistent cross-show patterns that support our core argument.

Stronger digital presentation

We made strong progress on the site by embedding YouTube clips, adding show cover images, and improving structure so viewers can follow the project more easily.

Team workflow

Using this coding and website workflow helped us move from broad ideas to a focused and usable final project.

Final Reflection

Final reflection: Overall, this project gives us a solid understanding of how emotional vulnerability works in reality TV today. We found that these moments are often shaped by production choices and then amplified through social media circulation. We still plan to tighten the design and trim excess wording, but the core argument, evidence, and direction are now clearly in place.
