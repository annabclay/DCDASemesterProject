Literature Review

Understanding the Academic Context: Reality TV, Authenticity, and Audience Engagement

Overview

Based on our research, reality TV is not simply “real life,” but a story that producers actively create. The concept of constructed authenticity explains how these shows are designed to feel real while being heavily controlled behind the scenes. Editing plays a major role in this process. Techniques such as confessionals, background music, and strategic clip sequencing shape audience emotions and guide interpretation. These choices are made to help viewers feel personally connected to contestants and storylines.

Social media also expands this process beyond the episode itself. Emotional scenes are clipped and reposted online, where audiences comment, react, and share them widely. This increases visibility and extends engagement across platforms. However, current scholarship still provides limited insight into which specific types of emotional moments generate the strongest engagement. That gap is central to our project, and our research focuses on identifying those patterns more directly.
