Research Methodology

How This Project Answers the Research Question

Our approach to this project was a qualitative content analysis of reality television, focusing on how emotional vulnerability is shown and used within episodes.

We sampled shows including The Bachelor, The Traitors, Love Island, and Dance Moms and selected specific clips that highlight emotional vulnerability: crying, personal storytelling, and conflict followed by resolution.

For each clip we analyzed where it appeared in the episode, how it was built up, and how editing techniques - music, pacing, and reaction shots - shaped audience interpretation and narrative impact.

Qualitative Analysis

Clip Analysis

For each clip, we documented the editing and production choices that shaped the moment.

  • Editing techniques: Cut pacing, reaction shots, and scene transitions
  • Audio design: Music, silence, and sound changes
  • Visual framing: Close-ups, camera angles, and shot selection
  • Narrative positioning: Where the scene appears in the episode and how it is built up
  • Emotional markers: Crying, voice changes, body language, and visible distress

Show Selection

We focused on The Bachelor, The Traitors, Love Island, and Dance Moms because they present vulnerability across formats: dating, strategy, social pressure, and youth performance.

  • The Bachelor: Romantic rejection and elimination pressure
  • The Traitors: Suspicion and betrayal
  • Love Island: Social pressure and public conflict
  • Dance Moms: Youth vulnerability and exploitation

Together, these shows let us compare how the same emotional pattern works across different genres.

Audience Response

To understand audience response, we examined social media engagement for the clips: comments, reposts, and circulation across platforms.

This revealed which types of emotional moments attracted the most interaction and how scenes persisted beyond the original broadcast.

  • Comments on clips and reposted moments
  • General interaction and circulation across social platforms
  • Which moments seemed most memorable or engaging

AI Use

How AI Helped

We also used AI tools to help with building our website and organizing our content. We would input a clip along with a description of what was happening and why it mattered to our research, and the AI helped turn that into structured writing for the site.

From there, we went back and edited everything to match our voice and make sure it was accurate. This process helped us use AI as a starting point, but not as the final product.

Using AI in this way showed us how helpful it can be for organization and clarity, but also how important it is to review and revise everything ourselves. It reinforced that AI should support our ideas, not replace them, which aligns with academic integrity guidelines.

Overall Takeaway

Overall, by combining qualitative analysis, social media observations, and AI-assisted organization, we were able to better understand how emotional vulnerability functions in reality TV.
